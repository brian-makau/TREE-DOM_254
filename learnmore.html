The Importance of Trees

Climate change

Climate change is the long-term alteration of temperature and typical weather patterns in a place.The main cause of climate change is burning fossil fuels such as oil, gas, and coal. When burnt, fossil fuels release carbon dioxide into the air, causing the planet to heat up. Some of the consequences of global warming include; Global warming, air pollution, water pollution and land pollution. There are, however, steps we can take towards reducing Climate change.They include; Using renewable energy, sustainable transportation, air pollution prevention, waste management & recycling and implementing the circular economy.

Reforestation

Reforestation is the process of replanting trees in areas that have been affected by natural disturbances like wildfires, drought, and insect and disease infestations — and unnatural ones like logging, mining, agricultural clearing, and development.

Why Reforestation

By planting trees in areas that have been degraded or deforested, reforestation helps the environment by guaranteeing, or accelerating the re-establishment of healthy forest structure by regrowing the forest canopy and preserving biodiversity within the ecosystem.

Benefits of Reforestation

  • Prevents floods and landslides by absorbing water and slowly releasing it via transpiration
  • Filters our drinking water through their roots
  • Helps to protect endangered species
  • Reduces the impact of global warming and climate change
  • Provides habitat for over 80% of the world’s terrestrial biodiversity
  • Prevents soil erosion by growing roots that hold the soil together.
  • Removes air pollutants by absorbing them into their leaves and bark
  • Acts as natural air conditioners by reducing ambient air temperatures by up to 8° Celsius
  • Helps carbon sequestrationby fixing atmospheric carbon dioxide in their leaves, trunks, and roots
